### How to find anyone's email, legitimately
1. Check the job post, HN post, founder bio, company website, GitHub profile, personal site, and X/LinkedIn profile.
2. Search exact phrases: `"founder name" email`, `"company.com" "hiring" email`, `"company" "HN" "email"`.
3. Use public email patterns only after confirming at least one company pattern, such as first@company.com or first.last@company.com.
4. Verify with a mail verifier before sending if the address is inferred.
5. Prefer public recruiter/founder emails, careers inboxes, portfolio talent forms, or LinkedIn/X DMs over guessing.
6. Never use hacked, leaked, private, or personal emails unrelated to work.

## Resume Tips

Anthropic's careers page says: "If you've done interesting independent research, written a thoughtful blog post, or contributed to open source, put that at the top of your resume." This means Ian's resume should lead with proof-of-work, not school.

Recommended top order:
1. Header: GitHub, portfolio, Substack, LinkedIn.
2. Proof-of-work highlights: juryrig, AI Advantage Sports, NBA CLV Dashboard, repo-health, kelly-js, OpenClaw contribution.
3. Summary: ML Engineer/Data Scientist with a completed B.S. in Information Science from USF and M.S. AI starting August 2026 at USF.
4. Technical skills.
5. Experience.
6. Education/certifications.

Specific edits:
- Add a "Selected AI Work" section above Experience.
- Put OpenClaw and Substack near the top, not buried.
- Make every bullet outcome-led: shipped, measured, deployed, evaluated, reduced, improved.
- For AI roles, avoid generic "passionate about AI." Say what was evaluated, what data moved, what shipped, and what changed.
- For frontier labs, emphasize open-source, independent writing, evals, reproducibility, and careful thinking.
- For startups, emphasize speed, ownership, customer workflow, and end-to-end shipping.
- For sports analytics, emphasize calibration, CLV-style evaluation, decision systems, and public product proof.
